# Session 9 Assignments (Foundation 5 + Challenge) — IUT1

## Foundational Problems

### P01: Computations in Quotient Rings
**Learning Objective**: Understand the operational rules in the quotient ring ℤ/nℤ  
**Problem Statement**: Prove that in Z/10Z, (7 + 8) * 4 = 60 = 0.  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem quotient_ring_calc : ((7 : ZMod 10) + 8) * 4 = 0`  
**Hints**:
- Compute using `norm_num` or `decide`
- Verify that 15 * 4 = 60 ≡ 0 (mod 10)

### P02: Zero Divisors in Quotient Rings
**Learning Objective**: Recognize the existence of zero divisors in quotient rings modulo composite numbers  
**Problem Statement**: In Z/15Z, demonstrate that both 3 and 5 are non-zero zero divisors. In other words, prove that 3 ≠ 0, 5 ≠ 0, yet 3 * 5 = 0.  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem zero_divisors_in_quotient : (3 : ZMod 15) ≠ 0 ∧ (5 : ZMod 15) ≠ 0 ∧ (3 : ZMod 15) * 5 = 0`  
**Hints**:
- Decompose each condition using `constructor`
- Verify using `decide` or `norm_num`

### P03: Unit Group in Quotient Rings
**Learning Objective**: Characterize the units in ℤ/nℤ  
**Problem Statement**: In Z/12Z, prove that 7 is a unit and its inverse is 7 itself.  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem unit_in_quotient : IsUnit (7 : ZMod 12) ∧ (7 : ZMod 12) * 7 = 1`  
**Hints**:
- Use `gcd(7, 12) = 1` as a condition for unithood
- Verify that 7² = 49 ≡ 1 (mod 12)

### P04: Isomorphism of Quotient Rings
**Learning Objective**: Understand that quotient rings with different representations are isomorphic  
**Problem Statement**: Prove that ℤ/(15) ≅ ℤ/15Z. More generally, verify that the quotient ring generated by n is isomorphic to ℤ/nZ.  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem quotient_isom_zmod : Nonempty ((ℤ ⧸ Ideal.span {15}) ≃+* ZMod 15)`  
**Hints**:
- Utilize `Int.quotientSpanNatEquivZMod`
- Apply the first isomorphism theorem

### P05: Frobenius Homomorphism
**Learning Objective**: Understand the Frobenius homomorphism over fields of characteristic p  
**Problem Statement**: In Z/5Z, prove that for any element x, x⁵ = x. (Fermat's Little Theorem)  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem fermat_little_theorem_five (x : ZMod 5) : x^5 = x`  
**Hints**:
- Use `ZMod.pow_card` or `ZMod.pow_card_sub_one`
- Note the special case for fields of characteristic 5

### Challenge Problems

### CH: Structure Theorem for Quotient Rings
**Learning Objective**: Understand the direct product decomposition and structure theorem for quotient rings  
**Problem Statement**: For n = 60 = 2² * 3 * 5, prove that Z/60Z ≅ Z/4Z × Z/3Z × Z/5Z. Furthermore, construct this isomorphism explicitly and show that (17 : Z/60Z) corresponds to (1, 2, 2).  
**Lean Goal**: `theorem quotient_structure_theorem : ∃ (f : ZMod 60 ≃+* ZMod 4 × ZMod 3 × ZMod 5), f 17 = (1, 2, 2)`  
**Hints**:
- This is a generalization of the Chinese Remainder Theorem
- Verify that 17 ≡ 1 (mod 4), 17 ≡ 2 (mod 3), and 17 ≡ 2 (mod 5)
