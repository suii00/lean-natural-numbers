# First Assignment (Basic 5 + Challenge) — Graph Theory

## Basic Problems

### S1_P01: Vertices and Edges in Graphs
**Learning Objective**: Understand the basic structure of SimpleGraph and verify edge symmetry  
**Problem Statement**: Prove that in a simple graph G, if vertices u and v are adjacent, then v and u must also be adjacent.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_P01 (G : SimpleGraph V) (u v : V) : G.Adj u v → G.Adj v u`  
**Hints**: 
1. The definition of `SimpleGraph.Adj` represents a symmetric relation
2. You may utilize `SimpleGraph.adj_comm`

### S1_P02: Basic Degree Properties
**Learning Objective**: Understand the concept of graph degree and be able to compute it  
**Problem Statement**: In a complete graph K₃ with three vertices, prove that each vertex has a degree of 2.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_P02 : ∀ v : Fin 3, (completeGraph (Fin 3)).degree v = 2`  
**Hints**: 
1. Review the definition of `completeGraph`
2. For finite cases, `simp` or `decide` can automatically prove the statement

### S1_P03: Determining Adjacency
**Learning Objective**: Be able to determine adjacency relationships in specific graphs  
**Problem Statement**: In a 4-vertex cycle graph C₄, prove that vertices 0 and 1 are adjacent, while vertices 0 and 2 are not adjacent.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_P03 : let C4 := cycleGraph 4; C4.Adj 0 1 ∧ ¬C4.Adj 0 2`  
**Hints**: 
1. Check the adjacency conditions for `cycleGraph`
2. The `decide` tactic can be used to make determinations

### S1_P04: Path Existence
**Learning Objective**: Understand the concept of paths in graphs  
**Problem Statement**: Prove that in any graph G, there exists a path of length 0 from a vertex to itself.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_P04 (G : SimpleGraph V) (v : V) : G.Reachable v v`  
**Hints**: 
1. Review the definition of `SimpleGraph.Reachable`
2. Use `SimpleGraph.reachable_refl`

### S1_P05: Complement Graphs
**Learning Objective**: Understand the concept and properties of complement graphs  
**Problem Statement**: For a graph G and its complement G', prove that if u and v are adjacent in G, then they are not adjacent in G'.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_P05 (G : SimpleGraph V) (u v : V) : G.Adj u v → ¬Gᶜ.Adj u v`  
**Hints**: 
1. The definition of complement graph `Gᶜ` inverts the edges of the original graph
2. You may use `SimpleGraph.compl_adj`

## Challenge Problems

### S1_CH: Degree Sequences of Graphs
**Learning Objective**: Understand the relationship between the sum of degrees and the number of edges in finite graphs (Handshake Lemma)  
**Problem Statement**: Prove that in a finite graph G, the sum of the degrees of all vertices equals twice the number of edges.  
**Lean Objective**: `theorem S1_CH [Fintype V] (G : SimpleGraph V) : (∑ v : V, G.degree v) = 2 * G.edgeFinset.card`  
**Hints**: 
1. Each edge contributes 1 to the degree of both its endpoints
2. Refer to `SimpleGraph.sum_degrees_eq_twice_card_edges`
