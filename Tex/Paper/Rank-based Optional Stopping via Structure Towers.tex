\begin{abstract}
We present a structural re-interpretation of the Optional Stopping Theorem (OST) for martingales using the concept of Structure Towers and Rank Functions. By formally identifying stopping times with rank functions on a stratified probability space, we transform the classical probabilistic statement into a structural property. We formalize this correspondence in Lean 4, demonstrating that the ``stopped process'' is canonically identified (definitionally equal via \texttt{abbrev} aliases) with a rank-restricted evaluation. This paper details the construction of the Structure Tower induced by a stopping time, the translation of probabilistic conditions to rank properties, and the subsequent derivation of the Bounded Optional Stopping Theorem. The formalization prioritizes a ``thin wrapper'' strategy, separating the high-level rank-theoretic arguments from the underlying measure-theoretic implementation.
\end{abstract}

% --- Section 1: Introduction ---
\section{Introduction}

The Optional Stopping Theorem (OST) is a cornerstone of martingale theory, asserting that the expected value of a martingale remains invariant under random stopping times, provided certain boundedness or integrability conditions are met. Traditionally, this result is presented purely within the language of measure theory. In this paper, we propose a categorical shift in perspective: we view the stopping time not just as a measurable random variable, but as a \textit{Rank Function} that induces a \textit{Structure Tower} on the probability space.

This section provides an overview of the paper's motivation and structure. We aim to bridge the gap between abstract structuralism and concrete probability theory. By defining a canonical correspondence between stopping times and structure towers, we show that the ``stopping'' operation is equivalent to a ``minimization'' operation in a rank lattice. This abstraction simplifies the conceptual burden of the proof and aligns naturally with formal verification strategies.

The primary contributions of this work are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Formal Equivalence:} We establish a correspondence between Structure Towers (nested subsets satisfying a covering property) and Rank Functions (maps to $\Nat$), formalized as a ``Normal Form'' theorem.
    \item \textbf{Stopping Times as Ranks:} We prove that any stopping time $\tau$ on a filtration $\Filtration$ induces a canonical rank function $\rank$, where the event $\{\tau \le n\}$ corresponds exactly to the $n$-th layer of the associated structure tower.
    \item \textbf{Rank-based OST:} We reformulate the Bounded Optional Stopping Theorem. We prove that the expectation of a martingale is invariant under ``rank restriction'' in the structure tower.
    \item \textbf{Lean 4 Formalization:} We describe a modular formalization of these concepts, distinguishing between a ``thin wrapper'' of rank-theoretic definitions and the robust measure-theoretic backend.
\end{itemize}

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:discrete} establishes the discrete-time setting. Section \ref{sec:ranktower} introduces the abstract theory of Structure Towers. Section \ref{sec:stopping_ranks} maps stopping times to these towers. Section \ref{sec:ost} proves the Rank-based OST. Section \ref{sec:lean} discusses the mechanization, and Section \ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

% --- Section 2: Discrete-time Setting ---
\section{Discrete-time Setting} \label{sec:discrete}

We restrict our attention to a discrete-time setting. Note that while the time index is discrete ($n \in \Nat$), the probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \Prob)$ is general (not necessarily finite or discrete).

Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \Prob)$ be a probability space.
\begin{definition}[Filtration]
A filtration $\Filtration = (\mathcal{F}_n)_{n \in \Nat}$ is a sequence of sub-$\sigma$-algebras such that $\mathcal{F}_n \subseteq \mathcal{F}_{n+1} \subseteq \mathcal{F}$ for all $n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Martingale]
A stochastic process $M = (M_n)_{n \in \Nat}$ is a martingale with respect to $\Filtration$ if:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $M_n$ is $\mathcal{F}_n$-measurable for all $n$.
    \item $\Ex[|M_n|] < \infty$ for all $n$ (Integrability).
    \item $\CondExp{M_{n+1}}{n} = M_n$ almost surely.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Stopping Time]
A map $\tau: \Omega \to \Nat \cup \{\infty\}$ is a stopping time with respect to $\Filtration$ if for every $n \in \Nat$, the event $\{\omega \in \Omega \mid \tau(\omega) \le n\}$ belongs to $\mathcal{F}_n$.
\end{definition}

% --- Section 3: Minimal Structure-Tower Definitions ---
\section{Structure Towers and Rank Functions} \label{sec:ranktower}

In this section, we define the abstract machinery of Structure Towers. We begin with a minimal definition depending only on the subset structure, and then derive the rank function as a consequence. This avoids circularity in the correspondence.

\begin{definition}[Structure Tower]
A \textbf{Structure Tower} consists of a carrier type $X$ and a sequence of subsets (layers) $\layer{n} \subseteq X$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Covering:} $\forall x \in X, \exists n, x \in \layer{n}$.
    \item \textbf{Monotonicity:} $i \le j \implies \layer{i} \subseteq \layer{j}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Rank Function]
A \textbf{Rank Function} is a map $\rank: X \to \Nat \cup \{\infty\}$. We say a rank function is \textit{covering} if $\forall x, \rank(x) < \infty$, in which case it can be identified with a map $\rank: X \to \Nat$.
\end{definition}

The following theorem establishes the bijection between these two structures.

\begin{theorem}[Rank-Tower Correspondence] \label{thm:rank_tower_corr}
There is a one-to-one correspondence (up to set equality for layers and extensional equality for functions) between Structure Towers and Covering Rank Functions ($\rank: X \to \Nat$).
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Rank to Tower:} Given $\rank$, define $\layer{n} = \{x \mid \rank(x) \le n\}$.
    \item \textbf{Tower to Rank:} Given a tower, define $\rank(x) = \min \{n \in \Nat \mid x \in \layer{n}\}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
For the ``Tower to Rank'' direction, fix $x \in X$. Let $S_x = \{n \in \Nat \mid x \in \layer{n}\}$. By the Covering property, $S_x$ is non-empty. By Monotonicity, $S_x$ is an upper set (if $n \in S_x$ and $m \ge n$, then $m \in S_x$). By the well-ordering principle of $\Nat$, $S_x$ has a unique minimum element. We define $\rank(x)$ to be this minimum. The inverse relationship follows directly from the definition of the minimum and the upper set property.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Canonical Form]
In our Lean formalization, we define a \texttt{StructureTowerWithMin} which includes the $\minLayer$ (rank) function as part of the data field for computational efficiency. Theorem \ref{thm:rank_tower_corr} justifies that this additional data is redundant but consistent: any Structure Tower induces a unique canonical $\minLayer$.
\end{remark}

% --- Section 4: Stopping Times as Ranks ---
\section{Stopping Times as Ranks} \label{sec:stopping_ranks}

We now bridge the gap between structure towers and probability theory.
Throughout this section, we primarily consider stopping times $\tau$ that are \textbf{bounded} (there exists $K$ such that $\tau \le K$). This ensures $\tau$ can be strictly identified with a covering rank function $\rank_\tau: \Omega \to \Nat$.

\begin{proposition}[Stopping Time as Rank]
Let $\tau$ be a bounded stopping time. Define $\rank_\tau(\omega) = \tau(\omega)$. Then the induced structure tower $\Tower_\tau$ satisfies:
\begin{equation}
    \layer{n}(\Tower_\tau) = \{\omega \in \Omega \mid \tau(\omega) \le n\}.
\end{equation}
Furthermore, the canonical rank derived from this tower is exactly $\tau$ itself.
\end{proposition}

Thus, the tower is probabilistically meaningful because each layer is $\mathcal{F}_n$-measurable.

\begin{remark}[Non-triviality of Adaptedness]
The structural definition alone yields subsets $\layer{n} \subseteq \Omega$. The crucial probabilistic content is that because $\tau$ is a stopping time, we have $\layer{n}(\Tower_\tau) \in \mathcal{F}_n$. This means the geometry of the structure tower is ``adapted'' to the filtration $\Filtration$.
\end{remark}

We define the \textbf{Rank-Stopped Process} $M^\tau$ (denoted \texttt{rankStoppedProcess} in the formalization) and the \textbf{Terminal Variable} $M_\tau$.
\begin{definition}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The process stopped at rank $n$: $M^\tau_n(\omega) := M_{\min(n, \tau(\omega))}(\omega)$.
    \item The value at the stopping time: $M_\tau(\omega) := M_{\tau(\omega)}(\omega)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

% --- Section 5: Rank Optional Stopping Theorem ---
\section{Rank-based Optional Stopping Theorem} \label{sec:ost}

We now present the main result: the Bounded Optional Stopping Theorem formulated via rank structures. We separate the argument into two parts: the stability of the stopped process (which holds for any stopping time relative to finite ranks) and the property of the terminal value (which requires boundedness).

\begin{theorem}[Rank Optional Sampling and Stopping] \label{thm:rank_ost}
Let $M$ be a martingale and $\tau$ a stopping time.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] \textbf{Rank-Consistency:} The rank-stopped process $M^\tau$ is a martingale. Consequently, for any rank $n \in \Nat$:
    \[ \Ex[M^\tau_n] = \Ex[M_0]. \]
    \item[(ii)] \textbf{Rank-Stopping (Bounded Case):} If $\tau$ is bounded (i.e., $\exists K, \tau \le K$), then for all $n \ge K$, $M^\tau_n = M_\tau$. Consequently:
    \[ \Ex[M_\tau] = \Ex[M_0]. \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof Sketch]
\textbf{Part (i):} We must show $\CondExp{M^\tau_{n+1}}{n} = M^\tau_n$.
\begin{align*}
    M^\tau_{n+1} &= M_{\tau \land (n+1)} \\
    &= \Ind{\tau \le n} M_\tau + \Ind{\tau > n} M_{n+1}
\end{align*}
Conditioning on $\mathcal{F}_n$:
\begin{align*}
    \CondExp{M^\tau_{n+1}}{n} &= \CondExp{\Ind{\tau \le n} M_\tau}{n} + \CondExp{\Ind{\tau > n} M_{n+1}}{n}
\end{align*}
Crucially, the term $\Ind{\tau \le n} M_\tau$ is $\mathcal{F}_n$-measurable. This is because it can be decomposed as a finite sum $\sum_{k=0}^n \Ind{\tau=k} M_k$, where each term is the product of an $\mathcal{F}_k$-measurable event ($\{\tau=k\} \in \mathcal{F}_k \subseteq \mathcal{F}_n$) and an $\mathcal{F}_k$-measurable variable ($M_k \in \mathcal{F}_k \subseteq \mathcal{F}_n$). Thus:
\begin{align*}
    &= \Ind{\tau \le n} M_\tau + \Ind{\tau > n} \CondExp{M_{n+1}}{n} \\
    &= \Ind{\tau \le n} M_\tau + \Ind{\tau > n} M_n \\
    &= M_{\tau \land n} = M^\tau_n.
\end{align*}
Since $M^\tau$ is a martingale starting at $M_0$, $\Ex[M^\tau_n] = \Ex[M^\tau_0] = \Ex[M_0]$.

\textbf{Part (ii):} If $\tau \le K$, then for any $n \ge K$, $\min(n, \tau) = \tau$. Thus $M^\tau_n = M_\tau$. Applying the result from (i) with $n=K$ yields $\Ex[M_\tau] = \Ex[M^\tau_K] = \Ex[M_0]$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Rank Expectation Invariance]
For a bounded rank function $\tau$, the expectation is invariant under the choice of layer $n$ in the rank tower (for $n$ sufficiently large).
\end{corollary}

\section{Lean Mechanization} \label{sec:lean}

The formalization of these results in Lean 4 adopts a ``thin wrapper'' strategy.

\subsection{Architecture}
The project is divided into distinct layers:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{\texttt{RankTower.lean}:} Pure order theory. Defines \texttt{StructureTowerWithMin} and proves the correspondence with rank functions.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{StoppingTime\_C.lean}:} Interface layer. Defines \texttt{stoppingTimeAsRank} and proves that the layer sets equal the stopping sets.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{Martingale\_RankStructure.lean}:} Computational core. It wraps existing Mathlib lemmas into rank terminology.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{OptionalStopping\_RankTheory.lean}:} High-level theory. States the OST purely in terms of ranks.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Benefits of the Thin Wrapper}
A key feature of our formalization is the use of \texttt{abbrev} to define rank-theoretic concepts. This ensures \textbf{definitional equality} with standard Mathlib concepts.

The following schematic code snippet illustrates how this allows us to reuse Mathlib's measure theory directly within our rank-theoretic proofs:

\begin{verbatim}
-- Schematic representation of the formal proof structure
theorem rankOptionalStopping_bounded ... :
  Integral (rankStoppedProcess M tau n) = Integral (M 0) := by
  -- Since rankStoppedProcess is definitionally equal to
  -- Martingale.stoppedProcess, we can apply the standard theorem:
  exact Martingale.stopped_process_integral_eq ...
\end{verbatim}

This strategy minimizes the friction between the high-level structural abstraction and the low-level measure-theoretic implementation.

% --- Section 7: Discussion ---
\section{Discussion} \label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented a structural approach to the Optional Stopping Theorem. By identifying stopping times with rank functions, we provided a geometric interpretation of the OST as an invariance property across the layers of a structure tower. While we focused on the bounded case, the correspondence paves the way for treating unbounded stopping times via limits in the structure tower.
